Syllabus

FLUID MECHANICS AND MACHINES

Course objectives
The objective of this course is to develop knowledge about the fluid properties, study of motion of fluid and related theory. It further focuses on the working principle of the fluid machines. After the completion of this course student will have good knowledge on the fluid motion behavior and different fluid machines. Any type industry cannot thrive without dependence on fluid and fluid machinery.

  1. Fluid properties and Fluid Statics (6 hours)
    1. Introductory concepts
    2. Definition of fluid
    3. Newtonian and Non-Newtonian Fluids
    4. Density
    5. Surface Tension
    6. Vapor Pressure
    7. Compressibility
    8. Cohesion and Adhesion
    9. Pascal’s law of pressure
    10. Pressure variation in a static fluid
    11. Manometers
    12. Forces on planes and curved surfaces

  2. Fluid Kinematics (6 hours)
    1. Description of fluid flow; one two and three dimensional flow
    2. Circulation and vorticity
    3. Rotational and irrotational flow
    4. Equation of stream line
    5. Velocity potential
    6. Stream function

  3. Basic Equation for fluid flow (6 hours)
    1. Continuity equation, turbulent and laminar flow, steady and unsteady flow
    2. Bernoulli’s equation and application, flow from a tank, venture flow, siphon flow
    3. Momentum equation and application

  4. Viscous Effects (10 hours)
    1. One Dimensional laminar flow, relationship between shear stress and velocity gradient
    2. Laminar flow between parallel plates
    3. Laminar flow in circular tubes; Reynolds number, velocity profile
    4. Laminar and Turbulent boundary layer flow, flow over flat plate, drag on immerged bodies
    5. Frictional resistance to a flow in pipes, Dacey-Weisbach equation, friction factor, use of Moody diagram, head loss in pipe flow
    6. Head losses, in bents, joint expansions, valves, loss coefficients
    7. Hydraulic and energy grade lines; systems including reservoirs, pumps and turbines
    8. Pipe flow networks; series and parallel combinations

  5. Hydraulic Turbines (10 hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. Hydroelectric Plants: Types and selection. Essential Components and their functions
    3. Water turbine:
    4. Classification and types of hydraulic turbines,
    5. Working principles of hydraulic turbines, components and their functions, characteristic and selection of hydraulic turbines
    6. Peltric Set, Improved Water Mill

  6. Pumps (6 hours)
    1. Classification and types of pumps
    2. Centrifugal and reciprocating pump, hydraulic ram,
    3. Working principles, components, functions, characteristics of centrifugal pumps,
    4. Pump as Turbine (PAT)

Practical

  1. Properties of fluids
    1. Measurement of fluid viscosity and density.

  2. Bernoulli’s principle verification.
  3. visualization of fluid flow (Laminar & Turbulent)
  4. Force of curved surfaces.
  5. Study of different types of pumps.
    1. Performance and characteristics pups.

  6. Performance of Pelton and Francis turbines.
