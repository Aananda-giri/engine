Syllabus

ENGINEERING ECONOMICS

Course Objective:
This course aims to provide sound and compressive coverage of engineering economics so that students can explain how the business operates, how engineering project decisions are made within the business and how engineering decisions can affect the bottom line (profit) of the firm. The course also targets to build a throughout understanding of the theoretical and conceptual basis upon which the practice of financial project analysis is built; all critical decision making tools- including the most contemporary, computer- oriented ones such as simultaneous techniques in risk analysis.

  1. Introduction (6 hours)
    1. Engineering Decision-Makers
    2. Engineering and Economics
    3. Problem solving and Decision making
    4. Intuition and Analysis
    5. Tactics and Strategy
    6. Engineering Economic Decision
    7. Interest rate, Simple interest, Compound interest
    8. Cash - flow diagrams, Personal loans and EMI Payment

  2. Present-worth comparisons (5 hours)
    1. Conditions for present worth comparisons
    2. Basic Present worth comparisons
    3. Present-worth equivalence
    4. Net Present-worth
    5. Assets with unequal lives, infinite lives
    6. Future-worth comparison
    7. Pay-back comparison

  3. Equivalent annual-worth comparisons (5 hours)
    1. Equivalent Annual-Worth Comparison methods
    2. Situations for Equivalent Annual-Worth Comparisons
    3. Consideration of asset life
    4. Comparison of assets with equal and unequal lives
    5. Use of shrinking fund method
    6. Annuity contract for guaranteed income

  4. Rate-of-return calculations and depreciation (6 hours)
    1. Rate of return
    2. Minimum acceptable rate of return
    3. IRR
    4. IRR misconceptions
    5. Cost of capital concepts
    6. Causes of Depreciation
    7. Basic methods of computing depreciation charges
    8. Tax concepts, corporate income tax

  5. Estimating and Costing (6 hours)
    1. Components of costs such as Direct Material Costs,
    2. Direct Labor Costs,
    3. Fixed Over-Heads,
    4. Factory cost,
    5. Administrative Over-Heads,
    6. First cost,
    7. Marginal cost,
    8. Selling price,
    9. Estimation for simple components

  6. Introduction, scope of finance, finance functions (4 hours)
    1. Statements of Financial Information:
    2. Introduction,
    3. Source of financial information,
    4. Financial statements,
    5. Balance sheet,
    6. Profit and Loss account,
    7. Relation between Balance sheet and Profit and Loss account.
    8. Simple Numerical.

  7. Financial ratio analysis (6 hours)
    1. Introduction,
    2. Nature of ratio analysis,
    3. Liquidity ratios,
    4. Leverage ratios,
    5. Activity ratios,
    6. Profitability ratios,
    7. Evaluation of a firm's earning power.
    8. Comparative statements analysis.
    9. Simple numerical.

  8. Financial and profit planning (6 hours)
    1. Introduction,
    2. Financial planning,
    3. Profit planning,
    4. Objectives of profit planning,
    5. Essentials of profit planning,
    6. Budget administration,
    7. Type of budgets,
    8. Preparation of budgets,
    9. Advantages, problems and dangers of budgeting.
    10. Introduction to Bench Marking of Manufacturing Operation.

References

  2. Thuesen H.G. Engineering Economy, PHI, 2002
  3. Tarachand, Engineering Economy, 2000.
  4. OP Khanna, Industrial Engineering and Management, Dhanpat Rai & Sons. 2000
