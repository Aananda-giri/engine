Syllabus

Human Reliability, Error and Factor in Aviation

Course Objectives:
To understand the Importance of Human Related Errors and its solution in aviation maintenance.

  1. Introduction (2 Hours)
    1. Background
    2. History
    3. Human Reliability, Error and Factor: Facts and Figure
    4. Terms and Definitions
    5. Human Reliability, Error and Factor: Useful Information

  2. Basic Mathematical Concept (5 Hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. Boolean Algebra Laws and Probability Properties
    3. Useful Definition
    4. Probability Distribution
    5. Solving First Order Differential Equation

  3. Introductory Human Factors, Reliability and Error Concepts (10 Hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. Human Factor Objectives and Man- Machine System Types and Comparisons
    3. Human Sensory Capacities and Typical Human Behavior and their corresponding design consideration
    4. Human Factors Related Formula
    5. Useful Human Factors Guidelines and Data Collection Sources
    6. Human Performance Effectiveness and Operator Stress Characteristics
    7. Operational Stressor and General Stress Factors
    8. Human Performance Reliability and Correct ability Functions
    9. Human Error occurrences reasons, consequences, ways and classification
    10. Human Reliability and Error Data Collection Sources and Quantitative Data

  4. Methods for Performing Human Reliability and Error Analysis in Aviation Maintenance (7 Hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. FMEA
    3. Man Machine Systems Analysis
    4. RCA
    5. Error Caused Removal Program (ECRP)
    6. Ishikawa (Fishbone Diagram) Cause and Effect
    7. Probability Tree Method
    8. Fault Tree Analysis
    9. Markov Method

  5. Human Error in Maintenance (6 Hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. Occurrence of Maintenance Error in Equipment life Cycles and Elements of a Maintenace
      Persons Time
    3. Maintenance Environment and Causes for the occurrence of Maintenance Errors
    4. Types of Maintenance Errors
    5. Common Maintainability Design Errors and Useful Design Improvement Guidelines to Reduce equipment errors
    6. Maintenance Work Order (Instructions)
    7. Maintenance Error Analysis Methods

  6. Human Factors in Aviation Maintenance (7 Hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. The need for Human Factors in Aviation Maintenance and Its Impact
    3. Human Factors in Aviation Maintenance: Challenge
    4. Practical Guide Human Factor: Aviation Maintenance
    5. Integrated Maintenance Human Factor Management System (IMMS)
    6. Training Program and Areas for Human Factor: Aviation Maintenance
    7. Regulatory Requirement: Human Factor in Aviation

  7. Human Error in Aviation Maintenance (8 Hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. Facts and Figures
    3. Causes of Human Error in Aviation Maintenance
    4. Major Categories of Human Errors in Aviation Maintenance and Inspection Tasks
    5. Common Human Errors in Aircraft Maintenance Activities
    6. Aircraft Maintenance Error Analysis Methods
    7. Maintenance Error Decision Aid ( MEDA)
    8. Guidelines for Reducing Human Error
    9. Case Studies in Human Error: Aviation Maintenance: British Airways, Continental Express Aloha Airlines.

  8. Safety (7 Hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. Facts and Figures
    3. Causes of Maintenance Safety Problems and Factors.
    4. Factors Influencing Safety Behavior and Culture in Maintenance Personnel
    5. Good Safety Related Practices and Measures
    6. Safety Question: Manufacturers
    7. Guidelines for Aircraft Engineering Designers to Improve Safety in Maintenance

  9. Mathematical Model for Performing Human Reliability and Error Analysis (5 Hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. Models for Predicting Maintenance Personnel Reliability in Normal and Fluctuating
      Environment
    3. Models for Performing Single System Maintenance Error Analysis
    4. Models for Performing Redundant Systems Maintenance
References:
  1. B.S. Dhillon, Human Reliability, Error, and Human Factors in Engineering Maintenance: with reference to aviation and power generation, CRC Press
  2. B.S. Dhillon, Human Reliability, Pergamon
