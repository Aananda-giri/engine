Syllabus

VERNACULAR ARCHITECTURE
AR 825 02

Course Objectives:

  1. To familiarize the students with fundamental of Vernacular architecture.
  2. To understand the underlying concepts, ordering principles and explore the rapidly disappearing vernacular architectural entities.
  3. To impart the knowledge of conversing and preserving the vernacular architectural building. 
  1. Introduction (1 hour)
    1. Introduction to vernacular architecture
      1. Society
      2. Culture
      3. Religion
      4. Architecture

  2. Evolution of Architecture(4 hours)
    1. Evolution and continuity
    2. Approaches and concepts
      1. Aesthetic aspects and Architectural aspects.
      2. Anthropological aspects and ethnographical aspects.
      3. Developmental and conservationist aspects.
      4. Historical and Folkloristic Aspects.

  3. Various Influencing  factors (6 hours)
    1. Physical factors and their influence on local architecture.
    2. Climate and ecological environment.
      1. Tropical, sub-tropical, monsoon, desert, maritime, montane, continental, arctic and sub-arctic and Mediterranean.
    3. Geography
      1. Location and sites (below ground, costal, slope, valley, plain or flat grass land etc.)
      2. Topography (Plain, slope & terrain etc.)
    4. Population and settlement pattern
      1. Growth
      2. Distribution and density (Sparse or Scattered & Dense)
      3. Migration and urbanization.
      4. Availability of building materials (Materials and building resource).
      5. Use of rocks and stones
      6.  Use of earth and clays
      7. Use of trees, grasses, palms, timber and thatch
      8. Use of animal products
      9. Use of manufactured products.
    5. Other miscellaneous materials and building recourses : Fibers, Cement, Glass, Tires Sheet, Metal etc.

  4. Social and Cultural attributes (2 hours)
    1. Social Organization, Cultural attributes and architecture
    2. Daily Practices.
    3. Family Occupation and economy
    4. Behavior Patterns of occupations.
    5. Social structures
      1. individual &interdependence
      2. various economic social stratifications & organization
      3. Leaders and authority

  5. Symbolism in Architecture (6 hours)
    1. Symbolism and Architectural Manifestation
    2. Decorated elements: Doorways, Facades, Columns, Walls, Floors, Openings and Other elements.
    3.  Inscriptions: Carpenters’ assembly marks, Date stones, Eng raved text, Masons marks, Sign writing, tile impression and other elements.
    4. Motifs: Swastika, Square(Mandala), Spiral geometry, Solar(sun), Star, Lunar, Cross, Crest, Suva lava , etc
    5. Rules, Systems and Rituals: Vastu  Vidya,  Feng  Shui, Divination & Sacrifice (Lela) ( Bali), Vastu Purusha Mandala etc., Proportion, symmetry, Golden Selection etc
    6. Symbolic Spaces etc: Cosmic, Fertility, Mythic domain, sacred boundary, etc.

  6. Building Technology and Production (2 hours)
    1. Building processes technology and architecture productions. (4 hrs)
    2. Designing to Dimensions, traditional crafts skills and building problems and solutions.
    3. Framing and infilling
      1. Structure and supports.
      2. Walls, Screen & fenesters etc.
      3. Flooring, roofing etc.    
    4. Services
      1. Circulations (Verandah, Balcony, Corridor, Hall, Gallery, Porch, Chowk, Lobby, Stair, Ladder, Lisno, Steps, Ramp, Footways, Bridge, etc)
      2. Cooking and Heating (Chulo, Bake, Oven, Againo, etc)
      3. Sanitation & Hygiene (Latrine ,Bath, Bathing Ghat, sauna, Stone, Spout  & Pond etc.
      4. Ventilation cooling and Lighting (courtyard, louvers, Water cooling, Aankhi jhyal, ventilation shaft,  wind tower, Water channel etc.
      5. Water supply (Dhunge dhaaro, Opi, Padhero, Canal Chanel, cistern jharu, irrigation.etc)

  7. Architectural Types (6 hours)
    1. Uses and functions of vernacular architecture ( 4 hrs)
    2. Domestic architecture
      Residential building, Hut Villa and Bungalow, etc.
    3. Sanctuaries and Temples
      Hindu temple, Buddhist Stupa, Church, Chapel, Cult House, Madrasa, Monastery, Mosque, Synagouge, Temple (haveli),Chorten, etc.
    4. Shrine & Funerary
      Cemetery, Grave shed , Grave Shelter, House deity (Vastu Purusha), Memorial, Tomb, Yard Shine, House Shrine, etc.
    5. Authority & status
      Citadel, Dzong, Guest House, Palace etc.
    6. Economic and social
      1. Economic: Bazaar, Shop, Counter, Store, Grain elevator, Granary (rice) Pottery kiln etc.
      2. Social: Drum tower (Menstruation/birth/Chaaupadi) Bride House, Dacha, Meeting hall, Dharma Sanstha, Tea house, Theater building etc.
    7. Out building (House) , Barn Cowshed (Goth), Corn culling, Granary, Pigeon Tower Storage Silo Thrashing, House, Watch Tower etc.

  8. Roles of Architecture in Community (3 hours)
    1. survival and continuity of vernacular architecture
    2. Collaboration of mordan techniques and vernacular techniques
    3. Roles of architects and mordan techniques
    4. Role of community

References:

  1. Encyclopedia of vernacular architecture  of the world by Paul Oliver vol 1&2
  2. The traditional architecture of Kathmandu valley by Wolfgang korn
  3. The brick &bull by Dr.Prof.S.R.Tiwari
  4. Temples of Nepal by Joel M.Boch Insacson
  5. Nepal Ka Kalatmak Jhyalharu by Dr. C. B. Joshi

Practical Assignments

  1. Inventory of existing vernacular architecture (10 hours)
    1. Domestic architecture (dwellings)
    2. Religious &culture building
    3. Scancharies & temples
    4. Shrine & funerary architecture
    5. Economic social & out building
  2. Project work (design work) (15 hours)
    1. Conservation & regeneration
    2. Renewable and retrofitting
    3. Design of new vernacular architects on selected topics
  3. Site visit/discussion (5 hours)
    1. Report preparation & presentation
