Syllabus

CONSTRUCTION MANAGEMENT

Course Objective:
The lessons in this course will address the pertinent issues and approaches to be considered during construction. At the same time the Architect is expected to realize his/her responsibility in undertaking and in the implementation of a construction project.

  1. Introduction to Construction Management (2 hours)                                                   
    1. Management: need and necessity
    2. Role of Architects in Construction Management

  2. Contract Administration (10 hours)
    1. Understanding Contract Administration and the Architects role
    2. Procurement Guidelines and selection and criteria
    3. Terms of Reference and Contract Agreements
    4. Bid and Contract Documents
    5. Progress report & billing

  3. Planning & Scheduling (10 hours)
    1. Introduction to planned approaches in construction
    2. Planning importance and techniques
    3. Bar or Gantt Charts
    4. Networking and it’s features: critical path diagrams
    5. S-curve

  4. Personnel Management (6 hours)
    1. Understanding the importance of human resources as a valuable sector
    2. Human Resource as a valuable sector
    3. Institution: objectives  and goals
    4. Organization approaches

  5. Material Management (6 hours)
    1. Understanding effective purchasing techniques, material handling and inventory control
    2. Economic Order Quantity: ascertaining savings
    3. Best Practice and Quality Assurance
    4. Proprietary rights

  6. Quality in Construction (4 hours)
    1. Importance of quality construction in design and construction works 
    2. Best Practice and Quality Assurance
    3. Principles of Inspection

  7. Maintenance management and Site Equipment (4 hours)
    1. Significance of managing site and construction equipment 
    2. Repair and Maintenance
    3. Maintenance Types                                                                                                                                                                                               
  8. Safety (6 hours)
    1. Understanding the need to create safe working environment
    2. Effects of physical, physiological and psychological impacts
    3. Benefits and Losses
    4. Standard Practices4
    5. Demolition Works 

  9. Cost Control (4 hours)
    1. Understanding the need for cost control
    2. Necessity and strategies

  10. Financial Management and Accounts (8 Hours)
    1. Introduction to financial planning and information
    2. Accounting Policy and Cash Flow
    3. Assets and Liabilities
    4. Financial Statements: Balance Sheet and income statements
    5. Auditing and Taxation

References:
