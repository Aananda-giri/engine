Syllabus

SUPPLY CHAIN MANAGEMENT

Course objective
After the completion of this course, students will be able to understand the basis of different industrial management functions like facilities planning, supply chain management, inventory management and control and business process re-engineering management.


Course Outline

  1. Introduction to supply chain Management (6 Hours)
    1. Objective and Benefits
    2. Supply chain stages and decision phases process view of a supply chain.
    3. Supply chain flows.
    4. Competitive and supply chain strategies.
    5. Achieving strategic fit.
    6. Expanding strategic scope.
    7. Drivers of supply chain performance.
    8. Framework for structuring drivers – Inventory, Transportation, Facilities, Information. Obstacles to achieving fit.

  2. Designing the supply chain network (4 Hours)
    1. Distribution Networking – Role, Design. Supply Chain Network (SCN)
    2. Role, Factors, Framework for Design Decisions.

  3. Facility location and network design (4 Hours)
    1. Models for facility location and capacity allocation.
    2. Impact of uncertainty on SCN – discounted cash flow analysis, evaluating Network design decisions using decision using decision trees.
    3. Analytical problems.

  4. Inventory management and control (4 Hours)
    1. Role of inventory in effective operation of production, distribution and maintenance system
    2. Forms of inventory, interaction of inventory with other systems like marketing, finance, research and production.
    3. Material requirement planning: estimation of demand and determination of inventory levels, safety stock and re-order point.
    4. Determination of inventory systems: economic order quantity, determination of continuous and discrete demand situations, quantity discount, joint ordering.
    5. Dependent and independent demand items, forecast of demand for slow and fast-moving demand items.
    6. Just-In-Time (JIT) inventory management systems.
    7. Exchange curve analysis and coverage analysis for multi-items.
    8. Probabilistic inventory systems: perpetual and periodic control systems,
    9. Store management: item classification, coding, storing and retrieval, issue policy, first-in-first-out (FIFO) or Last-in-first-out (LIFO)
    10. Inventory evaluation and stock verification

  5. Sourcing, transportation and pricing products (4 Hours)
    1. Role of transportation, Factors affecting transportation decisions.
    2. Modes of transportation and their performance characteristics.
    3. Designing transportation network.
    4. Trade-off in transportation design.
    5. Tailored transportation, Routing and scheduling in transportation.
    6. International transportation.
    7. Analytical problems.
    8. Role Revenue Management in the supply chain,
    9. Revenue management for: Multiple customer segments, perishable assets, seasonal demand, bulk and spotcontracts.

  6. Coordination and technology in the supply chain (6 Hours)
    1. Co-ordination in a supply chain: Bullwhip effect.
    2. Obstacles to coordination.
    3. Managerial levers to achieve co-ordination,
    4. Building strategic partnerships.
    5. The role of IT supply Chain,
    6. The Supply Chain IT framework,
    7. CRM, Internal SCM, SRM.
    8. The role of E-business in a supply chain,
    9. The E-business framework, E-business in practice.

  7. Emerging Concepts in supply chain management: Reverse Logistics (4 Hours)
    1. Reasons, Activities, Role.
    2. RFID Systems; Components, applications, implementation.
    3. Lean supply chains, Implementation of Six Sigma in Supply Chains.

  8. Production and Operation Management (POM) (10 hours)
    1. Production and operations strategy and interfaces: production/operation function and the organization, production/operation strategy, planning andcontrolling, the operations, POM & financial management, POM in manufacturing and service environments
    2. Plant and facilities: Location and design of the plant or facilities, layout of the facilities, equipment selection, maintenance of the facilities and equipment
    3. Production systems, including identification of technical, economic, social, human components and characteristics in the system.
    4. Forecasting techniques. Inventories, including role, measuring service level, inventory models and their application in distribution and manufacturing.
    5. Aggregate planning of production levels and inventories, including master plan, materials requirements planning (MRP), detailed scheduling and sequencing,assembly line balancing.
    6. Information and control systems for production operations. Project planning andcontrol.
