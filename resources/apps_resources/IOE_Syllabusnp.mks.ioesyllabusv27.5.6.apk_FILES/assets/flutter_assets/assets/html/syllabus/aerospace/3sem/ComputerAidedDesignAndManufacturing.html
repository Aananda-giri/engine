Syllabus

Computer Aided Design and Manufacturing

Course Objective:
The course provides an introduction to the fundamentals of drafting and computer-aided design with applications in aircraft and spacecraft design. Students will acquaint with various computer softwares for engineering design and familiarize with various types and designs that can be carried out using CATIA software. Students will be trained for designing of basic aeronautical parts. Basic training on drafting of the part, model and assembly design with the fundamental concepts of analysis of CAD designs. Students will team up with juniors and seniors to work on aerospace engineering design projects.

  1. Introduction (9 hours)
    1. Introduction to CAD
      1. I/O devices
      2. various graphics standards
    2. Coordinate systems
    3. Geometric Modeling
      1. Introduction
      2. Types of geometric modeling-wire frame- surface and solid modeling
      3. Wireframe entities- types of curves and its mathematical representation - line-circle- ellipse- parabola- Cubic spline- Bezier and B-spline (Only Basic treatment)
      4. Solid modeling entities - Solid modeling techniques- CSG and BREP - Operations performed in CSG and BREP - Extrude- sweep - linear and Nonlinear- revolve

  2. Graphic Concepts (2D and 3D) (9 hours)
    1. Transformations - translation- scaling- reflection- rotation
      1. Concatenated transformation
      2. Inverse transformation
      3. Hidden line removal - Z-Buffer algorithm- brief description of shading and color rendering techniques
    2. Manipulation and editing of entities
      1. Selection methods – dragging – clippingtrimming- stretching- offsetting- pattern- copying- deleting - regenerating- measuring
      2. Brief description of animation- types and techniques

  3. Software Packages and Recent Technology (9 hours)
    1. All about popular commercial solid modeling packages
      1. Salient features
      2. Technical comparison- modules and Tools available
      3. Brief outline of Data exchange standards
      4. Brief outline of feature technology
      5. Classification of features- design by features- applications of features- its advantages- and limitations

  4. Manufacturing in a competitive environment (9 hours)
    1. Automation of Manufacturing Process
    2. Numerical Control
    3. Industrial Robots
    4. Sensor Technology
    5. Application of Simulation in Manufacturing Process
      1. Forging
      2. Casting
      3. Stamping
      4. CNC 3-axis, 4-axis, 5-axis and 7-axis
      5. 3D Printing Technologies

  5. Analysis (9 hours)
    1. System Issues
    2. Types of Software
    3. Specification and Selection
    4. Manufacturing data systems
    5. Data Flow
    6. CAD/CAM Considerations
    7. Planning Flexible Manufacturing System (FMS) database

Practical
