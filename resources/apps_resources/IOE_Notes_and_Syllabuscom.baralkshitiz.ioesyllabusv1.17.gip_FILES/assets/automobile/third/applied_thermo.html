APPLIED THERMODYNAMICS AND HEAT TRANSFER
AM 501

Lecture   : 3
Tutorial   : 1
Practical : 3/2

Course objective:

Unit I: Applied Thermodynamics

  1. Boilers and condenser
    (6 hours)
    1. Steam Boiler: Classifications and working of boilers
    2. Boiler mountings and accessories
    3. Comparison between fire tube and water tube boilers
    4. Essentials of a good boiler, boiler efficiency
    5. Equivalent evaporation
    6. Classification of condenser, elements of a condensing plant
    7. Condenser performance parameters
    8. Comparison of jet and surface condensers
    9. Condenser vacuum and its efficiencies
  2. Psychrometry
    (6 hours)
    1. Psychrometric properties, Psychrometric
    2. Property calculations of air vapour mixtures by using chart and
    3. Psychrometric process – adiabatic saturation, sensible heating and cooling, humidification, dehumidification, evaporative cooling and adiabatic mixing
    4. Simple applications in refrigeration and air
  3. Vapour power cycles and steam engine
    (6 hours)
    1. Review of Rankine cycle
    2. Effect of pressure and temperature on Rankine cycle, super heat and Reheat cycle, Regenerative cycle
    3. Combined cycles
    4. Cogeneration
    5. Working and classification of steam engines
    6. Indicator diagram, saturation curve, missing quantity, heat balance.
  4. Air compressors
    (6 hours)
    1. Reciprocating air compressors types
    2. Construction and work, work of compression without clearance
    3. Effect of clearance and multistage
    4. Compressor efficiencies and mean effective
    5. Construction and working of rotary, vane and roots compressors
  5. Gas turbine and jet propulsion
    (6 hours)
    1. Gas turbine classification
    2. Review of Brayton cycle
    3. Principles of gas turbine
    4. Gas turbine cycles with intercooling, reheat and regeneration and their combinations
    5. Gas turbine efficiency and deviation of actual cycles from ideal cycles
    6. Principle of jet propulsion
    7. Turbojet and turboprop engines and their processes
    8. Introduction to rocket engine
  6. Steam turbines, steam and gas nozzles
    (8 hours)
    1. Classification, flow through impulse blades, velocity diagram, calculation of power output and efficiency, maximum blade efficiency of single stage impulse turbine, blade friction
    2. Flow through reaction blades, velocity diagram, calculations for power output, efficiency and blade height, comparison of impulse and impulse reaction turbines.
    3. Performance of steam turbines and its losses
    4. Flow through Convergent and convergent-divergent nozzles
    5. variation of velocity and heat drop, area and specific volume
    6. Choked flow, throat area, mass discharge
    7. Nozzle efficiency
    8. Effect of friction on nozzle
    9. Super saturated flow

Unit II: Heat Transfer

  1. Conduction
    (6 hours)
    1. Modes of heat transfer – conduction, convection and radiation
    2. General differential equation of heat conduction: Fourier’s law of conduction, one dimensional steady state heat conduction
    3. Conduction through plane wall, composite systems
    4. Unsteady heat conduction
    5. Lumped analysis – Use of Heisler’s chart.
  2. Convection
    (10 hours)
    1. Basic concepts
    2. Convective heat transfer coefficients, boundary layer concept, types of convection
    3. Forced convection, external flow: flow over plates, cylinders and spheres
    4. Internal flow, laminar and turbulent flow, flow over bank of tubes
    5. Free convection, flow over vertical plate
    6. Fins: types, selection and applications, governing equation
    7. Heat exchanger: classification, overall heat transfer coefficient, log mean temperature difference (LMTD) for parallel and counter flow, fouling factors
  3. Radiation
    (6 hours)
    1. Basic concepts
    2. Laws of radiation: Stefan Boltzman’s Law
    3. Kirchoff law, black body radiation, grey body radiation, shape factor algebra, electrical analogy
    4. Radiation shields
