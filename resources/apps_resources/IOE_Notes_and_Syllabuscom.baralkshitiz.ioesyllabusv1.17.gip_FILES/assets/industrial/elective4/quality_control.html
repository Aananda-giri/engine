QUALITY CONTROL AND MANAGEMENT
IE 74001

Lecture   : 3
Tutorial   : 1
Practical : 0

Course objective

After the completion of this course, students will be able to develop awareness and understanding of quality management and its importance for individuals, organization and society.

Course outline

  1. Quality management
    (5 hours)
    1. Concepts
    2. Definition
    3. Philosophy
    4. Interpretations
    5. Quality in Design
    6. Quality in Performance
    7. Quality Characteristics
  2. Quality And ISO Standards And Certification
    (6 Hours)
    1. ISO 9000 Family,
    2. Requirements,
    3. Quality Management Principles,
    4. Registration and Accreditation
  3. Total Preventive Maintenance (TPM)
    (6 Hours )
    1. Failure Patterns,
    2. Cost and Preventive Maintenance,
    3. Planning for Preventive Maintenance,
    4. Concept of Corrective and Preventive Maintenance,
    5. Concept of Six Sigma (Zero Defects)
  4. Safety and total quality management
    (6 hours)
    1. Implementing and Safety System,
    2. Safety Practices,
    3. Safety Standards (National and International).
    4. Core Concept, Practices, Benchmarking,
    5. Cost of Quality,
    6. Quality Process,
    7. Continuous Improvement,
    8. PDCA Cycle
  5. Quality control
    (2 Hours)
    1. Inspection,
    2. Quality Control and Quality Assurance
  6. Quality assurance methods and standards
    (4 hours)
    1. Product Quality Value Analysis,
    2. Classification of Defects Procedure,
    3. Specification of Inspection Method and Setting Standard Quality Levels
  7. Statistical quality control / statistical process control (SQC/SPC)
    (7 hours)
    1. Introduction,
    2. Concepts and Relevance,
    3. Tools and Techniques,
    4. Control Charts,
    5. Process Variation,
    6. accceptance Sampling by Attributes
  8. Acceptance sampling for variable, Taguchi method, method and loss function
    (4 Hours)
  9. Quality, productivity and efficiency
    (5 Hours)
    1. Quality Circle,
    2. Quality Organization,
    3. Quality Tools

Students will be divided into groups and will be asked to submit a case study report for their assignment marks.
