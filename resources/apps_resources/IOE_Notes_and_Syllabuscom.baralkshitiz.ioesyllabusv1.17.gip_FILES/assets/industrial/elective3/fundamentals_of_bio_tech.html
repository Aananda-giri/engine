FUNDAMENTALS OF BIO-TECHNOLOGY
IE 72504

Lecture   : 3
Tutorial   : 1
Practical : 3/2

Course Objective

After the completion of this course, students will be able to understand fundamentals of bio technology and apply on various production processes relating bio technology.

Course Outline

  1. Fundamentals of biotechnology
    (3 hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. Basic Concepts of Biotechnology
    3. Historic Evolution of The Biotechnology, Gene Expression, Stem Cells, Recombinant Dna Technology, Pcr, Trans-Genetics, Cloning
  2. Applications of biotechnology
    (3 hours)
    1. Animal Biotechnology, Plant Biotechnology, Medical Biotechnology,
    2. Environmental Biotechnology and Industrial Bio-Technology
  3. Bio technical production process for
    (5 hours)
    1. Food, Feed and Food Additives
    2. Therapeutical Proteins
    3. Technical Biopolymers
    4. Pharmaceuticals, Herbicides, Insecticides
    5. Organic Acids and Base Chemicals
  4. Fermentation process
    (4 hours)
    1. Aeration
    2. Agitation
    3. Temperature Regulation
    4. Filtration Method.
  5. Type of fermentation
    (4 hours)
    1. Solid State
    2. Submerged Fermentation
    3. Continuous Fermentation
    4. Immobilized Enzyme and Cell Bioreactors
  6. Process development
    (5 hours)
    1. Shake Flask Fermentation
    2. Down Stream Processing (DSP)
    3. Distingration of Cells, Separation, Extraction, Concentration and Purification of Products
  7. Production of microbial products
    (10 hours)
    1. Brief Account of the following products obtained by Industrial Microbiological Fermentation
    2. Alcohol
    3. Alcoholic Beverage – Beer
    4. Organic Acid - Citric Acid
    5. Entibiotic - Penicillin
    6. Amino Acids - Glutamic Acid
    7. Vitamin - B12
    8. Brief Account of Steroid Bio Transformation
  8. Enzyme bio-technology
    (6 hours)
    1. Characteristics of Enzymes - Amylases
    2. Industrial Uses of Enzymes - Detergents, Leather, Beverage, Food and Pharmaceutical
    3. Bioreactors for Enzyme Production – Stirred Tank, Membrane Reactors and Continuous Flow Reactors
  9. Fermented foods
    (5 hours)
    1. Fermented Foods - Yoghurt, Buttermilk, Tama, Gundruk, Cheese etc
    2. Microbial Foods - Single Cell Proteins (SCP), Single Cell Oils (SCO).

Practical

  1. Preparation of grape wine by spontaneous fermentation.
  2. Preparation of yogurt using pure culture.
  3. Production of fungal amylase be surface culture on wheat bran.
  4. Study of fomenters (demonstration only).
  5. Field visit to different laboratories facilities
