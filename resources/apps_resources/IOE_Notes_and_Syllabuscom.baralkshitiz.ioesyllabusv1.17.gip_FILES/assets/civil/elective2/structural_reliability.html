Lecture   : 3

Course Objectives:

The objective of this course is to provide knowledge of probabilistic design of civil engineering structures. This course includes the fundamentals of statistics and its application to civil engineering.

  1. Basic Statistics
    (8 hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. Probability theory
      1. Introduction
      2. Random events
      3. Random variables
      4. Functions of random variables
      5. Moments and expectation
      6. Common probability distributions
  2. Resistance Distributions and Parameters
    (10 hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. Statistics of properties of concrete, steel and other building materials
    3. Statistics of dimensional variations
    4. Characterization of variables, allowable stresses based on specified reliability
    5. Probabilistic analysis of loads: gravity loads, wind loads
  3. Basic Structural Reliability
    (12 hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. Computation of structural reliability
    3. Level 2 Reliability methods
      1. Introduction
      2. Basic variables and failure surface
      3. First order second moment methods (FOSM)
    4. Reliability based design
      1. Determination of partial safety factors
      2. Development of reliability based design criteria
      3. Optimal safety factors
  4. Monte Carlo Method
    (15 hours)
    1. Monte Carlo study of structural safety
    2. General, Monte Carlo method, applications
    3. Reliability of Structural system
      1. System reliability
      2. Modeling of structural systems
      3. Bounds of system reliability, reliability analysis of frames
