SURVEYING II
CE 554

Lecture   : 3
Tutorial   : 1
Practical : 3

Course Objectives:

To provide fundamental knowledge of land measurement and modern survey application such that students will be able to implement modern survey technique in map making and application in relevant to civil engineering projects.

  1. Traversing
    (7 hours)
    1. Needs and significance of traversing
    2. Specification for horizontal and vertical control of traverse
    3. Field works for traversing, traverse field notes
    4. Traverse computation for closed and link traverse, reduction of reading to angles, balancing of angles, computation of bearings and adjustment of bearings, computation of latitudes and departures, error of closure and relative precision, balancing of consecutive coordinates, computation of independent coordinates and plotting of traverse
    5. Traverse omitted measurements
    6. Field problems and instructions
  2. Tacheometry
    (5 hours)
    1. Principle of optical distance measurements
    2. Stadia method, Tangential method using staff vertical and horizontal distance using subtense bar
    3. Booking and plotting of details
    4. Sources of errors and precision of tacheometric survey
    5. Field problems and instructions
  3. Trigonometric Leveling
    (4 hours)
    1. Problems of heights and distance
    2. Reciprocal trigonometrical leveling
    3. It's significance and error ratio
    4. Determination of heights and distances of inaccessible objects
    5. Instruction on field works
  4. Contouring
    (4 hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. Establishment of controls
    3. Contour interval and characteristics of contour
    4. Methods of locating contours
    5. Interpolation of contours
    6. Uses of contour maps
  5. Orientation
    (4 hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. Analytical intersection and resection
    3. Two points and three point resection and their significance
    4. Instruction on field application
  6. Curves
    (8 hours)
    1. Types of curves and their uses
    2. Simple circular curves and their elements
    3. Calculation and setting out of simple circular curve by ordinate from long chord, offsets from tangent and deflection angle methods
    4. Geometry of transition curves and their elements
    5. Elements of composite curves and setting out techniques
    6. Equation of vertical curves and computation of reduced levels of points on curve
    7. Instruction on field application of curves
  7. Photogrammetry and Remote Sensing
    (5 hours)
    1. Introduction of photogrammetric as a branch of surveying
    2. Scale of vertical photograph
    3. Relief displacement
    4. Merits and limitation of photogrammetry
    5. Types of remote sensing
    6. Electromagnetic radiation
    7. Interaction of EMR with earth surface features
    8. Field application and instruction
  8. Field Astronomy and GPS
    (3 hours)
    1. Introduction, Definition of terms
    2. Geographical coordinate system
    3. Use of astronomy in surveying and mapping
    4. Introduction of GPS
    5. Components of GPS
    6. Working principles and,uses of GPS
    7. Instructions to field applications
  9. Total Station
    (3 hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. Features of Total Station
    3. Electronic data recording
    4. Summary of Total Station characteristics
    5. Field procedures for Total Station in Topographical Surveying
  10. Geographic Information System (GIS)
    (2 hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. Application of GIS to civil engineering projects

Tutorial:
(15 hours)

  1. Traversing
    Traverse computation i.e., including Reduction of reading to angles, balancing of angles, computation of bearings, calculation of consecutive coordinates and balancing of consecutive coordinates, calculation of independent coordinates, Finding the missing figures of traverse
  2. Tacheometry
    Distances and elevation computation from tacheometric observations and calculation of bearings, reduced levels and gradients from computed distances and angles
  3. Trigonometrical leveling
    Height and distance measurement practices for distant objects by applying various cases
  4. Contouring
    Interpolation practices from indirect method of contouring
  5. Orientation
    Coordinates calculation of unknown points by using resection and intersection processes
  6. Curves
    Calculation of various elements of simple circular curves, transition curves, composite curves and vertical curves for setting out procedures
