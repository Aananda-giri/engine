Lecture   : 4
Tutorial   : 2
Practical : 2/2

Course Objectives:

To provide knowledge of hydraulics which aims to impart the concept of water resources engineering and their application in the field of civil engineering for the design of various hydraulic structures

  1. Pipe Flow
    (9 hours)
    1. Introduction to pipe flow, distinguish between pipe and open channel flow.
      Reynolds experiment and flow based on Reynolds's number
    2. Laminar flow (Steady uniform incompressible flow in a circular pipe, shear stress, and velocity distribution)
    3. Head loss, Hagen Poisseuille equation.
    4. Turbulent flow. Shear stress development, Prandtl's mixing length theory, velocity Distribution, Darcy-Weisbach equation, Nikuradse's experiments.
    5. Resistance for commercial pipes, variation of friction factor with Reynold number, Colebrook-White equation, Moody's diagram
    6. Minor head losses in pipes (losses in sudden enlargement, sudden contraction, Exit loss, entry loss, losses in bends and losses due to different fittings).
    7. HGL and TEL lines
  2. Simple Pipe FIow Problems and their Solutions
    (5 hours)
    1. Three types of simple pipe flow problems and their solutions
    2. Pipe in series, Dupuit equation. Concept of equivalent pipe length
    3. Pipe in parallel, Different kinds of problems and their solutions
    4. Siphons and its application
    5. Computer programme coding for simple problems
  3. Three Reservoir Problems and Pipe Networks
    (6 hours)
    1. Introduction to three reservoir problems
    2. Solution procedures for possible different cases
    3. Introduction to pipe network problems and application
    4. Hardy-Cross method of solving of pipe networks problems
    5. Solution procedure by Hardy-Cross method for single and double loops of pipe networks with examples
    6. Computer programme coding for simple problems
  4. Unsteady Flow in Pipes
    (5 hours)
    1. Basic equations for unsteady flow: celerity, Euler's Equation and continuity equation
    2. water hammer and its effects
    3. Propagation of elastic wave in rigid and elastic pipe
    4. Pressure variation due to gradual and sudden closure of pipe Pressure variation at given point due to sudden closure of pipe.
    5. Relief devices against water hammer (different types of surge tank)
  5. Basics of Open Channel Flow
    (2 hours)
    1. Introduction to open channel flow and its practical application, differences between open and pipe flows
    2. Classification (natural and artificial channel, prismatic and non-prismatic channel, rigid boundary and mobile boundary channel).
    3. Geometric properties (depth of flow, area of flow, top width, wetted perimeter, hydraulic radius, hydraulic depth, bed or longitudinal slope, hydraulic slope, energy slope)
    4. Classification of open channel flow (Steady and unsteady; uniform and nonuniform; laminar and turbulent; sub-critical, critical and super-critical flow; gradually varied, rapidly varied and spatially varied flow)
  6. Uniform FIow in Open Channel
    (7 hours)
    1. Condition of uniform flow, expression for the shear stress on the boundary of channel
    2. Flow resistance equations. Darcy-Weisbach, Chezy and Manning equations and their relationship.
    3. Determination and factors affecting manning's roughness coefficient
    4. Velocity profile for laminar and turbulent flow, velocity distribution
    5. Velocity distribution coefficients and their application
    6. Conveyance, section factor, normal depth and hydraulic exponent for uniform flow computation
    7. Problems of uniform flow computation
    8. Best Hydraulic channel sections and determination of section dimensions (rectangular, triangular, trapezoidal and circular section)
    9. Computer programme coding for simple problems
  7. Energy and Momentum Principles in Open Channel Flow
    (10 hours)
    1. Energy principle, specific energy, specific energy curve, criteria for critical flow
    2. Critical depth computations for all kind of channel sections (prismatic as well as non-prismatic) and criteria for critical state of flow
    3. Discharge depth relationship
    4. Application of energy principle and concepts of critical depth concepts (channel width reduction, rise in channel bed, venture flume and broad crested weir)
    5. Momentum principle, specific force, specific force curve, criteria for critical state of flow, conjugate depth
    6. Computer programme coding for simple problems
  8. Non-uniform gradually varied flow (GVF)
    (8 hours)
    1. Introduction to GVF. Basic assumptions, Dynamic equation and its physical meaning
    2. Characteristics bed slopes (mild, critical, steep, horizontal and adverse).
    3. Characteristics and analysis of flow profiles
    4. Computation of GVF in prismatic channels by (graphical integration, direct integration and direct step and standard step methods)
    5. Computer programme coding for simple problems
  9. Non-uniform rapidly varied flow (RVF)
    (4 hours)
    1. Characteristics of RVF. Hydraulic jump as an energy dissipator
    2. Hydraulic jump in a horizontal rectangular channel. Relationship between hydraulic jump variables (conjugate depth, height of the jump, efficiency jump, length of the jump)
    3. Energy loss in jump
    4. Classification of the jump based on the tail water level and Froude number
    5. Practical application of jump at spillway toe, falls etc.
    6. Computer programme coding for simple problems
  10. Flow in Mobile Boundary Channel
    (4 hours)
    1. Introduction to rigid and mobile boundary channel
    2. Rigid boundary channel and its design principle (minimum permissible velocity approach)
    3. Definition of alluvial channel. Shear stress distribution on the channel boundary.
    4. Incipient motion condition
    5. Design of MBC by three approaches (the permissible velocity, tractive force and regime theory approaches)
    6. Introduction to Shied diagram and its application for designing MBC
    7. Formation of river beds based on the shear stress
