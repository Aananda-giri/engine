FARM MACHINERY AND EQUIPMENT
AE 551

Lecture   : 3
Tutorial   : 1
Practical : 2

Course Objective:

After the completion of this course students will be familiar with primary and secondary tillage and emplements, seeding, planting, harvesting, threshing, plant protection machines, equipment and there management.

  1. Objectives and Scope of Farm Mechanization
    (2 hours)
    1. Objectives of Farm Mechanization
    2. Scope and Limitations of Farm Mechanization in Nepal
    3. Policies and Related Strategies to Farm Mechanization in Nepal
  2. Tillage
    (3 hours)
    1. Definition and Objectives of Tillage
    2. Primary and Secondary Tillage
    3. Physical, Chemical and Biological Influences of Tillage
    4. Changing Views on Tillage
  3. Primary and Secondary Tillage Implements
    1. Mould Board Ploughs
      (3 hours)
      1. Types, Construction and Working Principle
      2. Accessories and Attachments
      3. Forces Acting on the Plough Bottom
    2. Disc Ploughs
      (3 hours)
      1. Types, Construction and Working Principle
      2. Accessories and Attachments
      3. Forces Acting on Plough Bottom
    3. Harrows
      (4 hours)
      1. Types, Construction and Working Principle of Harrows
      2. Functions of Harrows
      3. Others types of Harrows, their Selection and Use
      4. Forces Acting on Disk Harrow and their Analysis
    4. Rotary Tillage Tools and Implements
      (4 hours)
      1. Types, Construction and Working Principle of Rotavator, Stirring Plough and Auger Plough
      2. Advantages and Limitations of Rotary Tillage Tools
      3. Forces Acting on Rotary Tillage Tools
    5. Specialized Tillage Implements and Tools
      (2 hours)
      1. Sub‐Soiler and Chisel Ploughs
      2. Rider and Bundformer
      3. Puddler
    6. Tools and Implements for Intercultural Operations
      (3 hours)
      1. Objectives of Intercultural Operations
      2. Types, Construction and Working Principle of Cultivator
      3. Types of Intercultural Tools‐ sweep, shovel, hoe, rotary hoe etc.
      4. Horticultural Tools and Gadgets
    7. Measurement of Forces on Tillage Tools
      (3 hours)
      1. Dynamic Soil Properties Affecting Soil‐Tool Interaction
      2. Types of Dynamometers: spring, hydraulic, eddy current, strain‐gauge
      3. Draft, Unit‐Draft and Draft Power
  4. Seeding and Planting Machines
    (5 hours)
    1. Methods of Seeding and Planting and their Mechanization
    2. Types, Construction and Working Principle of Drills and Planters
    3. Types, Construction and Working Principle Paddy Trans‐planters
    4. Seed and Fertilizer Metering Devices in Drills and Planters
    5. Calibration and Field Adjustments in Drills and Planters
    6. Furrow Openers and Covering Devices in Drills and Planters
    7. Sugarcane and Potato Planters
    8. Recent Advances in Seeding and Planting Implements
  5. Machines and Equipment for Plant Protection
    (4 hours)
    1. Objectives of Spraying and Dusting
    2. Types of Sprayers and Dusters
    3. Working Principle and Components of Sprayers
    4. Working Principle and Components of Duster
    5. Safety in Handling Plant Protection Machines
    6. Selection and Calibration of Sprayers and Dusters
  6. Harvesting Machines
    1. Crop Harvesting Methods and their Mechanization
      (1 hours)
    2. Mowers
      (3 hours)
      1. Types, Working Principle and Constructional Details
      2. Functional Parameters of Mower Cutter‐bar
      3. Forces Acting on Cutter‐bar
      4. Adjustments and Balancing of Cutter‐bar
    3. Reapers and Windrowers
      (3 hours)
      1. Types, Working Principle and Constructional Details
      2. Reaper‐Binder
      3. Adjustments and Performance
    4. Harvesters for Other Crops
      (3 hours)
      1. Potato Digger‐ working principle and constructional details
      2. Groundnut Harvester‐ working principle and constructional details
      3. Sugarcane Harvester‐ working principle and constructional details
      4. Fruit Harvesting Machinery
      5. Cotton Pickers and Stripers
  7. Threshing Machines
    (4 hours)
    1. Threshing Mechanisms and their Mechanization
    2. Types Threshers, their Working Principles and Constructional Details
    3. Factors Affecting Thresher Performance
    4. Adjustments and Trouble‐Shooting in Mechanical Threshers
  8. Combined Harvesters
    (2 hours)
    1. Classification and Functional Components of Grain Combines
    2. Material Flow and Adjustments in Grain Combines
    3. Adjustments and Trouble‐Shooting in Combine Harvesters
  9. Chaff and Silage Cutters and Forage Harvesters
    (2 hours)
    1. Working Principle and Constructional Details
    2. Types of Cutter Heads
    3. Power Requirement and Cutting Energy
    4. Forage Blower
  10. Hill Agricultural Machinery
    (2 hours)
  11. Selection and Economics of Farm Machines and Equipment
    (4 hours)
    1. Selection Criteria of Farm Machines and Equipment
    2. Cost of Operation of Farm Machines‐ fixed and variable costs
    3. Management of Farm Machines for Optimum Performance
    4. Feasibility of Custom Hiring of Farm Machines and Equipment
