INSTRUMENTATION
EX 510

Lecture   : 4
Tutorial   : 1
Practical : 3

Course Objectives:

To provide the bask concept of instrumentation and knowledge of microprocessor based instrumentation system to interface different kinds of peripherals, circuit design techniques and instruments for a wide range of measurement problems.

  1. Instrumentation System
    (2 hours)
    1. Analog and Digital Signals and Systems
    2. Instrumentation System: Definition, Block Diagram, Component Descriptions
    3. Need for Electrical, Electronics, Pneumatic and Hydraulic Working Media Systems and Conversion Devices
  2. Theory of Measurement
    (6 hours)
    1. Static performance parameters - Accuracy, Precision, Sensitivity. Resolution and Linearity
    2. Dynamic performance parameters - Frequency response, Bandwidth, Response time
    3. Errors in measurement and Their Statistical Analysis
    4. Measurement of Voltage & Current (Moving Coil & Moving Iron Instruments)
    5. Measurement of Resistances (Low, Medium And High)
    6. AC Bridge (Wheatstone Bridge, Maxwell's Bridge, Schering Bridge)
  3. Transducer
    (8 hours)
    1. Introduction and Classification
    2. Applications of Transducers:
      1. Measurement of mechanical variables: Displacement, Strain. Velocity. Acceleration And Vibration
      2. Measurement of Process Variables - Temperature, Pressure, Level, Fluid
      3. Measurement of Bio-physical Variables Blood Pressure and Myoelectric Potentials
  4. Microprocessor Based System
    (2 hours)
    1. Basic Features, Advantages and Disadvantages of Microprocessor Based Instrumentation System
    2. Types: Open Loop and Close Loop
    3. PC Interfacing Techniques
    4. Review of Address Decoding, Mapping I/O and Interfacing of I/O ports and Memories
  5. Parallel Interfacing With Microprocessor Based System
    (6 hours)
    1. Methods of Parallel Data Transfer: Simple I/O, Simple Strobe I/O, Single & Double Handshake I/O
    2. 8255 as General Purpose Programmable Peripheral Device: Block Diagram, Mod. of Operation, Generation Control Words
    3. Interfacing Examples: I/O Ports, Keyboard, Printer, Seven Segment Display etc.
    4. Introduction to ISA and PCI bus
  6. Serial Interfacing With Microprocessor Based System
    (6 hours)
    1. Advantages of Serial Data Transfer on Parallel
    2. Basic Concepts in Serial I/O
      1. Interfacing Requirements
      2. Alphanumeric Codes
      3. Transmission Formats: Synchronous & Asynchronous, Simplex & Duplex, Rate of Transmission
      4. Errors and Error Checks in Data Communication
      5. Data Communication Over Telephone Lines
      6. Standards in Serial I/O: RS232, RS422, RS423
      7. Interfacing Examples 6.3 USB: Different Standards, Signals, Throughput, Protocols, Wireless USB and On-The-Go
  7. Interfacing ADC and DAC
    (5 hours)
    1. DAC: Characteristics, Weighted Register Type, R-2R Ladder Type
    2. ADC: Characteristics, Successive Approximation Type, Ramp Type, Dual-Slope Type, Flash Type
    3. Interfacing Different DAC and ADC to Microprocessor 7.4 Errors in ADC and DAC
  8. Data Acquisition System
    (2 hours)
    1. Data Acquisition System
    2. Data Loggers
    3. Data Archiving and Storage
    4. Bluetooth Devices and Characteristics
  9. Grounding and Shielding
    (4 hours)
    1. Outline for Grounding and Shielding, Safety
    2. Noise: Sources, Energy Coupling Mechanism, Prevention
    3. Grounding: Single-point Grounding, Ground Plane and Ground Loop
    4. Filtering: Bandwidth Minimization, Ferric Beads, Decoupling Capacitors, Line Filters, Isolators and Transient Suppressors
    5. Shielding: Definition and Types
    6. Protecting Against Electrostatic Discharge
    7. General Rules for Design
  10. Circuit Design
    (3 hours)
    1. Convening Requirement into Design
    2. Reliability and Fault Tolerance
    3. High Speed Design: Bandwidth, Decoupling, Ground Bounce, Cross Talk, Impedance Matching, Timing
    4. Low Power Design
    5. Reset and Power Failure Detection
  11. Circuit layout
    (3 hours)
    1. Circuit Boards and PCBs
    2. Component Placement
    3. Routing, Signal Traces: Trace Density, Common Impedance. Distribution Signals and Returns, Transmission Line Concerns, Trace Impedance and Matching, Avoiding Crosstalk
    4. Grounds, Returns, Shields,
    5. Connectors and Cables,
    6. Testing and Maintenance
  12. Software for Instrumentation System
    (3 hours)
    1. Types of Software, Selection and Purchase
    2. Different Software Models in metrics, Advantages and Limitations
    3. Risk Abatement and Failure Prevention: Issues, Development Plan, Safety and Reliability, Fault Tolerance
    4. Software Bugs and Testing
    5. Good Programming Practice
  13. Electrical Equipments
    (4 hours)
    1. Wattmeter: Types and Working Principle
    2. Energy Meter: Types and Working Principle
    3. Frequency Meter: Types and Working Principle
    4. Power Factor Meter: Types and Working Principle
  14. Case Study
    (6 hours)

    Example chosen from local industrial situations with particular attention paid to the basic measurement requirements, accuracy, and specific hardware employed environmental conditions under which the instruments must operate, signal processing and transmission, output devices:
    • Instrumentation for a power station including all electrical and non-electrical parameters.
    • Instrumentation for a wire and cable manufacturing and bottling plant
    • Instrumentation for a beverage manufacturing and bottling plant
    • Instruments required for a biomedical application such as a medical clinic or hospital.
    • Other industries can be selected with the consent of the subject teacher or needs.

Practical:

  1. Use of resistive, capacitive, inductive transducers and other types of transducer
  2. Review of assembly language programming and simple I/O interfacing with 8255
  3. Interfacing of LED, Seven Segment Display
  4. Interfacing of Motors
  5. Interfacing of different types of ADC
  6. Interfacing of different types of DAC
  7. Small Group Project: Design of simple microprocessor based instrumentation system
