Syllabus

POST HARVEST ENGINEERING

Course Objective:

  1. To acquaint and equip the students with different unit operations of food process industries.
  2. To acquaint and equip the students with the post harvest technology of cereals, pulses and oilseeds with special emphasis on their equipments.

  1. FoodProcessing [6 Hours]
    1. Scope and importance of food processing
    2. Principles and methods of food processing
    3. Processing of farm crops: Cereals, pulses, oil seeds etc. 
    4. Fruits and vegetables and their products for food and feed
    5. Processing of animal products

  2. Size  Reduction [5 Hours]
    1. Principle of size reduction,
    2. Grain shape
    3. Size reduction machines:
    4. Crushers, grinders, cutting machines, etc.
    5. Milling: modern rice milling; huller, Sheller, polisher
    6. Flour milling: steam conditioning
    7. Operation, efficiency and power requirement
    8. Rittinger's, Kick’s and Bond’s equation,

  3. Mixing [5 Hours]
    1. Theory of mixing (solid and liquid)
    2. Types of mixtures for dry and paste materials,
    3. Rate of mixing and power requirement,
    4. Mixing indices, its importance and applications 

  4. Separation [5 Hours]
    1. Theory of separation
    2. Mechanical, Electrostatic and Pneumatic separation
    3. Types of separators
    4. Sieve analysis: Actual and Ideal screens
    5. Capacity and effectiveness of screens
    6. Fineness modulus

  5. Filtration [4 Hours]
    1. Theory of filtration
    2. Types of filters (batch and continuous)
    3. Constant pressure and constant rate filtration
    4. Rate of filtration
    5. Pressure drop during filtration. 

  6. Material  Handling: [8 Hours]
    1. Scope & importance of material handling devices
    2. Different types of material handling systems; 
    3. Belt, Chain and Screw conveyor
    4. Bucket elevator, trajectory of particles
    5. Pneumatic conveying mechanism
    6. Design considerations of various material handling devices,
    7. Capacity and power requirement.

  7. Moisture  Content [4 Hours]
    1. Importance and applications moisture in processing
    2. Types of moisture in food
    3. Methods for determination
    4. Importance and applications of EMC
    5. Methods of its determination
    6. EMC curve and EMC model, water activity

  8. Drying & Storage [8 Hours]
    1. Theory of drying
    2. Mechanism of thin layer drying
    3. Critical moisture contents, falling rate and constant rate periods
    4. Deep bed drying and their analysis
    5. Shred’s and Hukill’s curve, Drying models
    6. Methods of drying grain drying,
    7. Types of dryers, Performance and energy utilization pattern
    8. Types and causes of spoilage in storage
    9. Control of temperature and Rh inside storage
    10. Modified and Controlled atmospheric storage and control of its environment, 
    11. Moisture and temperature changes in stored grains;
    12. Grain    storage    structures: Bukhari, Morai, Kothar, silo, bins, warehouses etc. 
    13. Economic, aspects of storage
