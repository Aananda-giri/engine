Syllabus

SMALL DAMS & RESERVOIRS
AE 785 01

Course Objective:
To familiarize and orient the students in theory, planning, technological development and management in engineering problem solving in the area of small dams and reservoirs.

  1. Introduction(2 hours)
    1. Physical Characteristics of Reservoir
    2. Types of reservoir
    3. Dead Storage, Live Storage

  2. Reservoir Capacity(16 hours)
    1. Selection and Investigation of Sites
    2. Area Elevation Curve
    3. Estimation of Reservoir Capacity
    4. Estimation of Sediment Flow and Reservoir Lift
    5. Reservoir sediment Control
    6. Water Losses from Reservoir and their Measurement
    7. Determination of Reservoir Yield by Mass Curve Technique
    8. Flood Routing

  3. Earth Dam(18 hours)
    1. Purpose and Types
    2. Site Selection and Investigation for Earth Dam
    3. Causes of Failure
    4. Criteria for Safe Design of Earth Dams
    5. Seepage Analysis
    6. Stability Analysis
    7. Stability of Foundation against Shear
    8. Seepage Control Measures
    9. Purpose and Location of Spillway
    10. Design of Spillway
      1. Design of Straight Drop Spillway
      2. Design of Overflow/Emergency Spillway
      3. Energy Dissipation below Spillway

  4. Water Harvesting(3 hours)
    1. Importance and Principle of Water Harvesting
    2. Water Harvesting Techniques
    3. Flood Water Harvesting

  5. Farm Ponds(6 hours)
    1. Types and Components of Farm Ponds
    2. Determination of Pond Capacity
    3. Design of Embankment
    4. Seepage through Embankment
    5. Design of Mechanical and Emergency Spillway
    6. Pond Protection, Maintenance and sanitation
