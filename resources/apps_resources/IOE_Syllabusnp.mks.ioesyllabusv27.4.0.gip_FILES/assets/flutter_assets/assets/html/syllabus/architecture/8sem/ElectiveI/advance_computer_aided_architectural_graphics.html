Syllabus

Advance Computer Aided Architectural Graphics
AR 765 04

Course objective

  1. To introduce the course in the study of Computer Aided Drafting (CAD) with regard to Architecture.
  2. Helps people to believe in the possibility of the design.
  3. To acquaint the student with the range of graphic tools which are available for conveying architectural notions.
  4. To prepare students for the professional life with tools that is used in AEC industry.
  1. Basic Modelling [6 Hours]

    2. A basic class in the study of three-dimensional (3-D) computer aided design for Architecture. The course will use modelling technology where sketch up will be used as the primary software to teach how to model, surface, light and animate the design of a building and its surrounding environmental context.

    1. Modelling room for visualization
    2. Composing scene and saving the camera view
    3. Introduction to texture, materials and components for photo real rendering
    4. Starting introduction to photo images and rendering
    5. Walkthrough and flyover
    6. Presentation in Layouts

  2. Building Information Modelling (BIM) [12 Hours]

    3. A basic class in the study of Building Information Modelling with Autodesk Revit Architecture as tool. The course will enable student to grasp the idea BIM learn modelling and information handling with Revit architecture

    1. Introduction to Building Information Modelling (BIM)
    2. Revit architecture Basics 
      1. Lesson on User interface
      2. Lesson on Revit Elements and Families
      3. Lesson on Working with templates
    3. Commencing a design: Lesson on Levels and grids
    4. Building a Model
      1.  lesson on creating a floor plan
      2. Lesson on adding walls
      3. Lessons on doors and windows
      4. Lesson on editing tools
    5. Working with component families
    6. Viewing the model
      1. Managing views and controlling the visibility
      2. Working with section and elevation vies
    7. Working with dimensions its constraints
    8. Developing building Model: Working on creating and modifying floors ceiling and roof
    9. Presentation of the building model

  3. Graphics [8 Hours]

    4. Application of the techniques architectural illustrators uses to render architectural presentation drawings. This course focuses on the organization and preparation of a portfolio showcasing a student's creative work in preparation for employment in the field. Computer photo editing and printing, paper and digital portfolio preparation, and the implications of using a portfolio in the interview process for employment are emphasized. The course will use Adobe Photoshop / Autodesk Impression as the primary software.

    1. Photoshop workspace basics:Introduction to tools, rulers, grids, guides, plug-ins and preferences
    2. Color and tonal adjustments:understanding color adjustments with adjusting matching, replacing and mixing
    3. Retouching and transformation
      1. Adjusting crop, rotation and retouching images
      2. Transforming objects
    4. Working with layers and filters
    5. Photographic special effects
      1. Blurring, sharpening, vigenetting
      2. using color for emphasis
      3. replacing skys
      4. Replication photographic filters
      5. layer masking for collage
      6. Adding depth of field
      7. Stitching paranomas together
      8. Sharpening techniques
    6. Showing to the client

  4. Presentations [4 hours]

    5. Various graphic conventions and techniques will be presented and the rationale behind their use explained. Students will explore visual composition and organization, document assembly methods and various reproduction and image management techniques. Applications for impressive presentation. The course will use Adobe Indesign, skecthup layout as the primary software.

    1. Adobe Indesign workspace basics for presentations
    2. Sketchup layout basics for presentations
