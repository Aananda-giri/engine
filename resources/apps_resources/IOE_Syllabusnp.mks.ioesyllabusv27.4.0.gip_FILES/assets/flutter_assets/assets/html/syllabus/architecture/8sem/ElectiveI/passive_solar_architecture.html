Syllabus

PASSIVE SOLAR ARCHITECTURE
AR 765 03

Course Objective:

  1. To introduce the concept of Passive Solar Architecture in relation to architectural design
  2. To deal with the use of the specific Passive Solar Architecture in design and construction to produce  comfortable, energy efficient and environment friendly buildings.
  1. Concept of Passive Solar Architecture [4 hours]
    1. Introduction, Objectives & Importance
    2. History of passive solar architecture
    3. Concept of low energy passive building

  2. Concept of Solar Radiation [2 hours]
    1. Solar Energy & its type – Direct, Diffuse & Reflected
    2. Solar Radiation – Absorption & Reflection on Earth
    3. Geometry of Solar movement
    4. Solar Chart & its uses in passive design

  3. Concept of Climatology [2 hours]
    1. Climate, weather & season
    2. Micro & Macro, Micro Climatic factors
    3. Site & Urban climate
    4. 3.4 Climatic data & interpretation

  4. Passive Building Design Concept [2 hours]
    1. Passive heating system
    2. Passive cooling system
    3. Passive ventilation system

  5. Passive Solar Heating System [5 hours]
    1. Passive Solar gain – site planning, orientation, colour, texture, material & technology
    2. Solar gain - Direct gain, Indirect gain & Isolated gain
    3. Direct solar gain – through window, glass wall
    4. Indirect solar gain – through thermal storage wall, floor & roof
    5. Thermal storage wall –Trombe wall, water wall, traditional brick wall
    6. Thermal storage roof –Solar chimney, Roof trap, Roof pond
    7. Thermal storage floor–traditional composite mud flooring
    8. Isolated solar gain – Green house, Solarium, sunspace, etc

  6. Passive Solar Cooling system [5 hours]
    1. Control of Solar radiation – site planning, orientation, material & technology
    2. Solar radiation control techniques - Natural & Artificial
    3. Natural – using colour, texture, vegetation & water body
    4. Artificial - shading by various External & Internal devices
    5. External devices – vertical, horizontal, geometric overhang, chhajja etc
    6. Internal devices – curtain, venetian blind, special glass etc.
    7. Shading by texture – wall & roof
    8. Roof shading – green roof, inverted pots, vegetation, water spray etc

  7. Passive heating and cooling by ventilation system [2 hours]
    1. Stack effect, Air vent
    2. Courtyard effect
    3. Wind tower & EAT system

  8. Traditional passive buildings & settlement of Nepal [2 hours]
    1. Traditional residential buildings of Kathmandu
    2. Clusters & Courtyard type building & settlement
    3.  Traditional Earth shelters

  9. Active Solar Heating & Cooling [2 hours]
    1. Solar water heater, floor heating system
    2. Hybrid system - Natural convective loop, water & air loop

  10. Project work with the application of passive solar system [4 hours]
    1. Design of a Residence in one of the climatic zones of Nepal-Terai, Hilly, Kathmandu valley & Mountain
    2. Climatic data- Solar radiation, Temperature, wind pattern, Humidity, Precipitation
    3. Micro-climatic data- local wind flow, Solar access to the site, vegetation, water body
    4. Objective – Design response with comfort & energy efficiency
    5. Site analysis- Topography, orientation, Solar envelope
    6. Analysis of orientation, site & space planning &  building configuration
    7. Application of passive system for heating & cooling
    8.  Selection of materials & construction technology with low K value, U value
    9. Construction of Openings, walls, roofs, floors etc.
    10.  Thermal performance of building envelope- wall, roof, opening
    11. Analysis - Heat gain & loss by external, internal & ventilation load
    12. Conclusion
