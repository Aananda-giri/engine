Syllabus

DRAFTING – I

Course Objective:

To familiarize with the use of drafting instrument and introduce drawings by the exercises through the concepts and principles of orthographic and parallel projection to develop drafting skill.

  1. Preliminary drawing skill (6 hours)
    1. Introduction of drawing and its classification:  Artistic drawing, engineering drawing, Geometrical drawing:  a) Plane geometrical drawing, b) Solid geometrical drawing, Architectural, Civil, Computer, Electrical, Electronics and Mechanical drawings.
    2. Drafting equipments and their use, importance of accuracy, drawing sheet format and layout of drawing sheet etc.
    3. Skill development practice in drafting:  Different types of line, line weights etc.; convention for lines, materials and features, texture of different materials.
    4. Lettering and its types:  Proportional base, style base & stroke and direction base.
    5. Scale and dimensioning:  Its types and systems.
    6. Reproduction of geometrical and building drawings at different scales.
    7. Geometrical drawings:  Point, line bisection, perpendicular line, parallel line, trisection of line, bisection of angle, trisection of angle etc.
    8. Inscribing and describing of circle about a triangle, square and polygon etc.
    9. Construction of square, different types of polygons, external and internal tangents, arc tangents, special curves, conical sections and other geometrical constructions.
    10. Tracing in pencil & ink medium.

  2. Theory of Projection (1 hour)
    1. Introduction
    2. Types of projection:
      a) Orthographic projection
      b) Pictorial projection.
    3. Pictorial projection: 
      a) Axonometric
      b) Oblique
      c) Perspective.

  3. Orthographic Projection (2D drawing) (3 hours)
    1. Introduction
    2. System of three planes
    3. Principles of orthographic projection
    4. Projection of point in different planes
    5. Projection of lines of different position in relation to the projection planes
    6. Projection of rectangular surfaces of different position in relation to the projection planes
    7. Projection of solids (cube, hexagonal prism, cylinder, cone, pentagonal pyramid and sphere) in different projection planes
    8. Projection of point and lines on the surface of solid figures
    9. Projection of three dimensional objects in different planes (conversion of isometric views into orthographic projection)

  4. Pictorial Projection (3D drawing) (2 hours)
    1. Isometric Projection:  Isometric scale, isometric axes, isometric lines, non-isometric lines, isometric planes
    2. Conversion of orthographic views into isometric projection and drawing
    3. Conversion of geometrical and architectural drawing into axonometric projection 45°- 45°, 30° - 60° (Metric projection)

  5. Different views sections and surfaces (3 hours)
    1. Auxiliary views and its type and projection
    2. Sectional views and its types and projection (section of solids)
    3. Intersection of surfaces and its methods of projection
    4. Development of surfaces and its methods of projection
