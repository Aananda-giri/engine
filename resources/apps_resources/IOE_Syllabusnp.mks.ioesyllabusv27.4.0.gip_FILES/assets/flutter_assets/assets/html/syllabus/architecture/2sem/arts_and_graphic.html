Syllabus

ARTS & GRAPHIC

Course Objective:
To introduce fundamental of architectural elements and forms, geometrical forms, principles of composition, three dimensional composition, color application and presentational techniques in architectural drawing and understanding architectural graphics as a means of visual communication.
  1. Scope of study and its importance (2 hours)
    1. Arts and drawings as a means to communicate ideas, and graphic thinking.
    2. Elements and Forms (Points, line, plane, solid, natural and man-made artifacts, geometrical forms and objects)

  2. Interpenetration of different forms (2 hours)
    1. Prismatic, pyramidal, cylindrical & conical forms
    2. Spherical & other forms
    3. Building design forms

  3. Basic principles and Composition  (2 hours)
    1. Tone (Bright and dull tone, tonal value, high, medium and dark)
    2. Texture / Pencils HB, H, B (use and stress)
    3. Compositional development of presentation (Texture, tree, bushes/shrubs, human figure, vehicles and clouds etc.)
    4. Composition (Balance, Rhythm, Scale, Unity, Harmony, Contrast, Monotony, Focal point, Hierarchy etc.

  4. Color theory & its applications (3 hours)
    1. Color (Color scheme, color wheel and its properties, uses and effects etc.)
    2. Use of color in architectural buildings (Exterior and Interior)
    3. Composition of collage, CD cover, magazine cover in different visual media and techniques

  5. Architectural rendering development skill in different mediums (2 hours)
    1. Rendering in architectural building (Exterior)
    2. Rendering in architectural building (Interior)

  6. Preparation of a set of presentational drawings of a simple Building using appropriate rendering techniques (4 hours)
    1. Floor Plans including furniture layout.
    2. Elevations
    3. Sections
    4. Site Plan
    5. Shadow construction in plans and elevations
    6. Perspective drawing (Exterior & Interior)
