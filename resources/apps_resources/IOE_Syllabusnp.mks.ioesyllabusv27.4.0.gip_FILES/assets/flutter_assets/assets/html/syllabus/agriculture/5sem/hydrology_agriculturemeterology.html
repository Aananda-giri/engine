Syllabus

HYDROLOGY AND AGRICULTURAL METEOROLOGY

Course Objective:
The objective of this course is to give the students an understanding of the complex interactions between the waters of the land and atmosphere, the science of hydrology, and the role that hydrology plays in agricultural and urban system.

  1. Introduction to Hydrology and Hydrologic Cycle [2 hours]
    1. Hydrology as Science of Water
    2. Scope and Application to Agricultural Engineering
    3. Hydrological Cycle and its Components
    4. Hydro-meteorological Development in Nepal

  2. Agricultural Meteorology  [6 hours]
    1. Definition and its importance in Agricultural Engineering
    2. Use of meteorological information in agricultural planning and management
    3. Agro-climatic factors affecting agriculture - radiation, temperature, vapor pressure and wing measuring instruments
    4. Energy balance at the surface and at different crop canopies
    5. Evaporation and evapotranspiration

  3. Precipitation  [6 hours]
    1. Causes, Occurrence and Forms of Precipitation
    2. Rainfall Measurement
    3. Presentation of Rainfall Data
    4. Test of Consistency of Rainfall Records
    5. Network Design
    6. Estimation of Missing Rainfall Data
    7. Areal Averaging of Rainfall
    8. Intensity-Duration – Frequency Analysis
    9. Depth-Area-Duration Curve
    10. Spatial and Temporal Variability of Rainfall in Nepal

  4. Infiltration and Percolation  [3 hours]
    1. Mechanics of Infiltration and Percolation
    2. Factors Affecting Infiltration and Percolation
    3. Infiltration, Infiltration Capacity
    4. Infiltration Indices
    5. Measurement of Infiltration and Percolation Rates
    6. Horton's Equation

  5. Evaporation and evapotranspiration [5 hours]
    1. Mechanics of Evaporation and Evapotranspiration
    2. Potential and Actual Evapotranspiration
    3. Factors Affecting Evaporation and Evapotranspiration
    4. Estimation of Evaporation and Evapotranspiration by experimental field measurement and by climatic approach including Penman's Equation

  6. Runoff and Stream Flow  [5 hours]
    1. Definition and Components of Runoff
    2. Factors Affecting Runoff
    3. Estimation of Runoff
    4. Rainfall- Runoff Relationship
    5. Stream Gauging: types and site selection
    6. Rating Curves
    7. Stream Flow Measurement using direct and indirect methods
    8. Use and Calibration of Current Meter

  7. Hydrograph Analysis  [7 hours]
    1. Hydrograph: concept and components
    2. Development and use of stream flow hydrograph
    3. Unit hydrograph: assumptions, limitation and uses
    4. Derivation of unit hydrograph
    5. Derivation of runoff hydrograph using effective rainfall hyetograph
    6. Synthetic unit hydrograph, instantaneous unit hydrograph and dimensionless unit hydrograph

  8. Statistics and Probabilities in Hydrology [6 hours]
    1. Frequency and Probability Concepts-plotting position formulae
    2. Frequency Analysis and Recurrence Interval
    3. Frequency Distribution Functions and their Application: normal, log- normal, gumbel distribution, log Pearson type-III
    4. Selection of Distribution Type
    5. Confidence Limits
    6. Risk, Reliability and Safety Factor

  9. Hydrology of Floods  [5 hours]
    1. Definition, causes and effects of floods
    2. Estimation of floods peaks for gauged and ungauged catchments
    3. Flood prediction and design floods
    4. Flood routing, hydrologic routing
    5. Flood control measures
