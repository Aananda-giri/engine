Syllabus

SOLID BIO FUEL

Course Objectives:
To know different types of solid biofuels resources and technology applied for different end uses and sector. To know about wood and non woody biomass resources. To design of cook stove.To know scope and application of solid biofuel in Nepal.

  1. Introduction of gaseous biofuels (12 hours)
    1. Traditional and modern application of solid biofuels
    2. Share of solid biomass fuels in the national, regional energy consumption and newly emerging trend
    3. Contribution of biomass solid fuel to the local socio-economy through income and employment generation
    4. Wood and non wood solid biomass fuel resources
      1. Direct fuelwood from forest
      2. Direct fuel from non forest land
      3. Indirect and recovered fuelwood
      4. Non wood solid fuel (Animal residue, agricultural residue )
    5. Assessment of current state of cooking fuel
    6. Characteristics and calorific value of different solid biofuels

  2. Solid biofuel conversion and utilization technology (18 hours)
    1. Cook stove
      1. Design and operation of different kinds of ICS
      2. Design and operation Institutional Cook Stove
      3. Commercial design of fuel wood stove
      4. Techno economic aspect of different ICS
      5. Subsidy policy of ICS
    2. Pyrolysis and charcoal making
      1. Charcoal production
      2. Medium CV gas
      3. Bio-oil production
    3. Briquetting and Pelltising
      1. Existing technology of bio-briquette
      2. Raw material for briquette
      3. Briquette production technology
      4. Techno-Economic analysis of production
      5. Prospects of commercial bio-briquette
    4. Biomass gasification
    5. Dendro thermal power generation
    6. Biomass fuel based boiler, kilns and furnace

  3. Organic Solid Waste Management Technology (9 hours)
    1. Land fill
      1. Introduction
      2. Operations
      3. Impacts
    2. Incineration
      1. Introduction
      2. Technology
      3. Consequences
    3. Composting
      1. Introduction
      2. Comparison of aerobic and anaerobic processes
      3. Operating process
      4. Advantages and disadvantages
    4. Anaerobic digestion
      1. Introduction
      2. Process
        1. Process stages
        2. Configuration
      3. Feedstocks
      4. Products
      5. Applications

  4. Municipal Waste Management (3 hours)
    1. Production of municipal solid wastes (Generation, Composition, Collection, Segregation, Transportation, Processing, Disposal)
    2. Contents, quantities, disposal by landfill
    3. Recovery of energy by biomethanation
    4. Incineration and pelletisation

  5. Industrial and agricultural solid waste management (3 hours)
    1. Generation, Composition and Disposal
    2. Recovery of energy by biomethanation
    3. Techno-economics

Practical:

  1. Determination of efficiency of different type of ICS
  2. Briquette making process and determining efficiency
  3. Gasification
