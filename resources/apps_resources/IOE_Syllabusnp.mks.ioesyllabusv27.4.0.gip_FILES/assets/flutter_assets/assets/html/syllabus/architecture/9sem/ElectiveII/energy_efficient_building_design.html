Syllabus

ENERGY EFFICIENT BUILDING DESIGN
AR 825 05

Course Objective:

  1. To introduce the basic concepts of energy efficient building design in relation to architectural design
  2. To deal with the use of the energy efficient building design in design and construction to produce comfortable, energy efficient and environment friendly green buildings.
  3. To be able to incorporate energy efficiency criteria in building design in different climatic zones
  4. To use the concept of energy efficient design for the final thesis project
  1. Concept of Energy Efficient Building [4 hours]
    1. Introduction and Objectives
    2. Importance of Energy Efficiency
    3. Concept of Energy Efficient Building
    4. Concept of Green & LEED Building

  2. Energy Situation & Consumption [2 hours]
    1. Energy crisis 
    2. Energy situation in Nepal & World
    3. Energy resource in Nepal – Solar, biomass, hydro, etc
    4. Energy consumption pattern - Household, Commercial, Industrial etc

  3. Energy Efficient Building Design [3 hours]
    1. Study of climate & climatic data
    2. Site planning
    3. Passive solar design
    4. Building material
    5. Building technology
    6. Integration of Renewable Energy systems 
    7. Energy system – Lighting, HVAC
    8. Water Efficiency & Solid Waste Management

  4. Study of Climate & Climatic data [2 hours]
    1. Climate, micro-climate, climatic zone
    2. Solar radiation, Temperature, Humidity, Wind, Precipitation

  5. Site Planning [1 hour]
    1. Site Analysis - Topography, Solar access, Wind flow, Site Orientation, Vegetation,

  6. Passive Solar Design   [3 hours]
    1. Passive solar heating, Passive solar cooling, Passive solar heating & cooling
    2. Passive solar heating - Direct, Indirect, Isolated gain, color, texture
    3. Passive solar cooling – Shading, ventilation, color, texture
      Shading by Neighboring buildings, Vegetation, Overhangs, louvers, texture facade, movable blinds and curtains
      Courtyard effect, Air cooling, Earth cooling, EAT, Evaporation cooling
    4. Combined Passive heating and cooling
      Trombe wall, Roof ponds, Solar chimney, Wind tower, etc.
  7. Building Material [1 hour]
    1. Embodied energy of material
    2. Reuse & recycle material
    3. Healthy environment friendly material

  8. Building Technology [2 hours]
    1. Traditional efficient technique – wall, floor, roof, Rammed earth
    2. Modern efficient technique - CSEB, Rat-trap bond, cavity structure,
    3. Inco- panel, UPVC door, windows, green concrete, etc.

  9. Energy system [3 hours]
    1. Energy efficient Lighting – Day lighting, Artificial lighting
    2. Day lighting – Atrium, Sky light,
    3. Artificial lighting – CFL, LED lighting,
    4. HVAC, EAT system

  10. Integration of Renewable Energy systems [2 hours]
    1. Solar PV panel
    2. Solar water heater
    3. Solar space heating system
    4. Bio-mass and others

  11. Water Efficiency & Solid waste management [1 hour]
    1. Water-efficient toilet, Sensor taps
    2. Rainwater harvesting, Waste water treatment
    3. Biogas/bio-digester

  12. Case study of Energy Efficient Buildings - Zero energy building & other buildings   [2 hours]

  13. Project work with the application Energy Efficient Building Design [4 hours]
    1. Design of a building in one of the climatic zones of Nepal
    2. Climatic data- Solar radiation, Temperature, Wind pattern, Humidity, Precipitation
    3. Micro-climatic data- local wind flow, Solar access to the site, vegetation, water body
    4. Objective – Design response with energy efficiency & environment friendly
    5. Site analysis- site efficiency with topography, orientation, Solar envelope
    6. Application of energy-efficient design
    7. Selection of materials & construction technology
    8. Integration of Renewable Energy systems
    9. Lighting & HVAC system
    10. Water efficiency & Solid waste management
    11. Conclusion
